April Newsletter
APRIL 15, 2018
 

2018 Spring Work Day
The Board requests that as many residents who can come along to help on this annual Work Day, Saturday April 21. Plan is to meet at the pool parking lot at 9.30am. Work we have planned includes laying “play safe” mulch at the playground to help with the safety for the kids. Also, we plan to spread pine straw on ground adjacent to the play area and at the Park (The Green) at the end of the Parkway. If you have a wheel-barrow, please bring it along. REMEMBER – all who help will have their name included in the draw for 2019 FREE DUES at the annual HOA meeting,
 

Easter Egg Hunt
This event was very successful again thanks to the work put in by Suzy Richardson and Traci Smith, along with others. The kids really enjoyed it on a beautiful morning. “Thanks” go out to all helpers.
 

Spring Neighborhood Yard Sale
For all who wish to participate, our Spring Neighborhood Yard Sale which will take place on Friday 4th and Saturday 5th of May – sale hours: 9:00am to 1:00pm. each day. The HOA will support the advertising and place a large banner at the entrance to our subdivision. Hope all participants have a good day.
 

Landscape Improvements
Over the last 3 weeks or so we have had remedial work done to improve several common areas by having turf laid. The Board wishes to thank the following who have either provided water, done the actual watering, or both, and provided equipment. So, thank you – Tania Ogea and Rick Hein on the Terrace, Jesse Gonzalez on the Drive and Emer Coughlin and Allison Brownlee on the Parkway. Without their help the Bermuda grass would not have survived.
 

Swimming Pool

Play Area Climbing & Slide

Street Parking
When this neighborhood was established the planners included double garages and drives to accommodate parking without interfering with traffic movements. The Board is constantly being advised of inconsiderate parking, not only by visitors but residents as well. Many cars are street parked just for personal convenience with disregard as to how it affects neighbors. The Board requests that we all check how we park and how it affects others and change habits to be more considerate to both close neighbors and other residents in the neighborhood.
